01

Why the ceiling is the point

NBA games are genuinely hard to call. The model running into a wall at 63% is the most reassuring thing about it.

THE WALL

~63% is the real ceiling

With leakage-free rolling features, single-game prediction tops out near 63%. Las Vegas itself only reaches ~66–68%. I pushed the model harder — bigger, deeper, less regularized — and it would not move. That's the signal ceiling of the data, not a tuning failure.

THE LEAK TEST

A high score would be a red flag

If this had returned 90%, the right response is suspicion, not celebration — a feature secretly encoding the final result. Earlier versions leaked through head-to-head and season win-percentage stats and inflated the score. Cleaning them out brought it back to an honest 63%.

THE FIT

Train ≈ Test, so it generalizes

Training accuracy (62.8%) and held-out test accuracy (62.9%) sit within a tenth of a point. The model isn't memorizing the past; it's learning patterns that transfer to seasons it never saw. Heavy dropout and weight decay keep it there.
